08 UI Design Style

Liquid Glass

Apple’s 2025 material — reflects and refracts light like glass while flowing like liquid. The evolution of glassmorphism.

Emerged: Jun 2025 (WWDC, iOS 26+)New · surgingCutting-edge · a11y debate ongoing

👁At a glance

Liquid Glass is Apple’s new design material unveiled at WWDC in June 2025, applied across iOS 26, iPadOS 26, macOS Tahoe 26, watchOS 26 and tvOS 26. It adds real-time specular highlights and fluid motion to glass’s optical reflection/refraction, so controls float above content and reflect their surroundings. A refined evolution of glassmorphism — but early versions drew usability criticism (legibility, tap targets), so apply it carefully.

🧬Key characteristics

Reflective/refractive material

Reflects and refracts surrounding content in real time

Fluid motion

Buttons/tabs stretch and respond like liquid

Specular highlights

Shimmering edge highlights that react to light

Floating controls

Hover over content, morphing shape/visibility by context

⚖️Strengths vs Limitations

Presented neutrally. Weigh them against your project's goals.

✅ Strengths

  • Perfectly aligned with Apple’s ecosystem — cutting-edge, premium feel
  • Reflection/depth/motion give strong immersion and materiality
  • Unified design language across five OSes = high consistency
  • Elegantly separates content and control layers
  • The most advanced 2026 trend — strong differentiation

⚠️ Limitations

  • Early legibility issues (low text contrast over translucency) flagged by NN/g
  • Shrinking tap targets and context-appearing controls can hurt predictability/discoverability
  • Real-time rendering burdens performance and battery
  • Depends on latest Apple OS/hardware — hard to reproduce on web/other platforms
  • Pre-maturity — guidelines and best practices keep shifting

🎯When it fits / when to avoid

👍 Good fit when

  • Native Apple apps on iOS 26+ / macOS Tahoe
  • Premium products centered on Apple’s ecosystem
  • Showcases/demos emphasizing the newest trend
  • Media apps needing clear control/content layering

👎 Avoid when

  • Accessibility/legibility-first services
  • Products centered on older devices or non-Apple platforms
  • Performance/battery-sensitive low-end contexts
  • Productivity tools requiring stable, predictable UX

🧾Outsourcing brief (Upwork)

Liquid Glass — Outsourcing brief (Upwork)

  • For native Apple, require use of SwiftUI’s built-in Liquid Glass APIs (more stable than reimplementing)
  • For web reproduction, expect <code>backdrop-filter</code> + SVG refraction + specular highlight, and require performance validation
  • Put NN/g’s flagged issues (text contrast, shrunk tap targets, control predictability) into the QA checklist/contract
  • Mandate <code>prefers-reduced-transparency</code>/<code>reduced-motion</code> support and an opaque fallback
  • Because it depends on latest OSes, specify minimum supported versions and fallback UX
