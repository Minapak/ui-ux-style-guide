Material Design 3 Material You

Google's open design system — dynamic color from the user's wallpaper, large rounded shapes, tonal elevation and clear, accessible components.

Released: 2021 (Material You)Mature · systemUbiquitous on Android & Google apps

👁At a glance

Material Design 3 (aka Material You) is Google's 2021 evolution of Material Design. Its signatures are dynamic color — tonal palettes generated from a seed color or the user's wallpaper — plus larger corner radii, tonal surfaces and elevation, refreshed components and strong built-in accessibility. It is a full design system, not just a look, backed by official libraries for Android (Jetpack Compose), Flutter and the Web.

⚖️Strengths vs Limitations

Presented neutrally. Weigh them against your project's goals.

✅ Strengths

  • Comprehensive, thoroughly documented design system
  • Official cross-platform libraries speed up delivery
  • Strong accessibility built in — contrast, touch targets, motion
  • Consistent, familiar UX for Android users
  • Theming and personalization via dynamic color

⚠️ Limitations

  • Can look generically “Google” without customization
  • Dynamic color adds real theming complexity
  • A heavy system to fully adopt end-to-end
  • Opinionated — hard to stray far without fighting it
  • Web support is less mature than Android

🎯When it fits / when to avoid

👍 Good fit when

  • Android apps and cross-platform (Flutter) products
  • Teams wanting a ready-made, accessible system
  • Products that value personalization and theming
  • Large apps needing consistency at scale

👎 Avoid when

  • The brand needs a highly distinctive, custom look
  • Tiny projects where the full system is overkill
  • The team can't maintain tokens and theming over time
  • You must match a non-Material platform (e.g., strict iOS HIG)

🧾Outsourcing brief (Upwork)

Material Design 3 — Outsourcing brief (Upwork)

  • Specify M3 and which library up front — Jetpack Compose, Flutter or Material Web
  • Provide the seed / brand color and let the tool generate the tonal palette as tokens
  • Require documented component usage — don't hand-roll what M3 already ships
  • Set touch-target and contrast acceptance criteria (M3 accessibility)
  • Decide light / dark and dynamic-color scope before build starts
