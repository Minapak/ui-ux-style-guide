Corporate Memphis Alegria / flat people

The friendly flat-illustration look of Big Tech — faceless people with long bendy limbs, bright flat color blocks and simple geometric shapes.

Popularized: ~2017–2021 (Big Tech)Common · widely usedAlso known as Alegria

👁At a glance

Corporate Memphis — also called “Alegria” after Meta/Facebook’s illustration set, or “flat people / Big Tech” illustration — is a flat vector illustration style built from simplified, usually faceless human figures with disproportionately long, bendy limbs, bold blocky colors and simple geometric shapes. It was popularized ~2017–2021 by tech companies (Facebook, Google, Slack and countless startups) for friendly, approachable brand illustration that scales easily. It borrows the 1980s Memphis movement’s geometric playfulness in a tamed, corporate form — and has become both ubiquitous and widely criticized as generic.

⚖️Strengths vs Limitations

Presented neutrally. Weigh them against your project's goals.

✅ Strengths

  • Friendly, approachable and inclusive-reading
  • Cheap and fast to produce and scale
  • Flexible illustration system across a whole brand
  • Abstract figures sidestep photo-casting and diversity issues
  • Pairs naturally with flat / minimal UI

⚠️ Limitations

  • Heavily overused — “every startup looks the same”
  • Can feel impersonal or corporate
  • Widely mocked as a cliché
  • Offers little brand differentiation
  • Can read as inauthentic or generic

🎯When it fits / when to avoid

👍 Good fit when

  • SaaS / tech marketing, onboarding and empty states
  • Brands needing illustration at scale, fast
  • Friendly consumer or B2B products
  • Startups wanting a quick, cohesive illustration system

👎 Avoid when

  • Brands wanting strong differentiation or craft
  • Premium / luxury or serious, somber tone
  • Authentic photography matters more
  • Audiences already fatigued by the cliché

Corporate Memphis — Outsourcing brief (Upwork)

  • Commission a reusable illustration system — consistent figures, palette and shapes — delivered as SVG components
  • Define the palette and figure proportions as tokens up front
  • Keep illustrations decorative — put essential info in real text with alt text, not the artwork
  • Ensure figures read as abstract and diverse
  • Add a distinctive twist (custom palette, motif) so it doesn’t look like every other flat-people site
