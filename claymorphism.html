04 UI Design Style

Claymorphism

A soft-clay 3D look — chunky rounded shapes with inner and outer shadows create a puffy, friendly feel.

Emerged: 2021 (coined by Hype4)Growing · nichePairs well with illustration & 3D

👁At a glance

Claymorphism renders elements as if molded from clay — plump and soft. Large radii, an outer drop shadow, and two inner <code>inset</code> shadows (one light, one dark) create the 3D clay texture. Combined with bright pastels and 3D illustration, it’s popular for friendly, playful brands.

⚖️Strengths vs Limitations

Presented neutrally. Weigh them against your project's goals.

✅ Strengths

  • Friendly, playful mood — great for youthful/lifestyle brands
  • Strong depth makes elements easy to distinguish
  • Bright colors evoke joy and positivity
  • Blends naturally with 3D illustration and emoji
  • Higher contrast than neumorphism, so friendlier for a11y

⚠️ Limitations

  • Playful tone doesn’t suit serious or professional services
  • Chunky forms eat space, lowering information density
  • Multiple shadows make CSS complex and can cost performance
  • Trend-driven — risk of looking dated quickly
  • Producing consistent 3D assets needs design resources

🎯When it fits / when to avoid

👍 Good fit when

  • Bright services for kids, education, games, playful fintech
  • Onboarding and illustration-led landing pages
  • Marketing using emoji / 3D icons
  • App-icon and illustration systems

👎 Avoid when

  • Finance, legal, B2B where trust/authority matters
  • Data- and table-heavy productivity screens
  • Minimal / luxury brand tones
  • Performance-sensitive low-end targets

🧾Outsourcing brief (Upwork)

Claymorphism — Outsourcing brief (Upwork)

  • Key spec: large <code>border-radius</code> + one outer drop shadow + two inner <code>inset</code> shadows (light & dark)
  • Give the dev a pastel palette and 3D icon assets (or a library)
  • Performance: cap shadow layers at 3–4 per component
  • Define radius/shadow values as design tokens for consistency
  • Require a separate dark-mode palette since pastels lose contrast
