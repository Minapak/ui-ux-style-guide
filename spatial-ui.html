10 UI Design Style

Spatial UI

An interface placed in depth and 3D space, beyond the flat plane — the design language of spatial computing led by Apple Vision Pro (visionOS).

Emerged: since 2024 (visionOS)Emerging · growingFuture-facing for AR/VR & spatial computing

👁At a glance

Spatial UI places the interface in a 3D space with depth rather than on a 2D plane. Translucent panels float in the space around the user, and interaction/hierarchy are expressed through gaze, hands, depth (z-axis), shadow and parallax. Apple Vision Pro’s visionOS defined this language, and the concept is spreading to ‘depth-aware’ layered UI on web and mobile too.

🧬Key characteristics

Depth / z-axis

Elements placed front-to-back to form 3D spatial feel

Floating panels

Translucent glass panels hover in space

Light, shadow, parallax

Depth-based shadows and parallax create dimensionality

Gaze & hand input

Assumes spatial input like eye contact and pinch gestures

⚖️Strengths vs Limitations

Presented neutrally. Weigh them against your project's goals.

✅ Strengths

  • Delivers a new experience through strong immersion and presence
  • Uses depth as hierarchy to express information structure intuitively
  • Optimized for the next-gen platform of spatial computing / AR-VR
  • Organizes content naturally, like physical space
  • Lends the brand a future-facing, innovative image

⚠️ Limitations

  • Heavy dependence on dedicated hardware (headsets) limits reach/accessibility
  • High difficulty and cost to design/develop 3D spatial interaction
  • Long sessions can cause fatigue or motion sickness
  • Conventions and best practices are still forming
  • Ported to web/mobile it often becomes only a ‘depth imitation’

🎯When it fits / when to avoid

👍 Good fit when

  • visionOS / AR/VR immersive apps
  • 3D product viewers and spatial data visualization
  • Fields where spatial understanding matters (education, medical, design)
  • Future-facing concepts and showcases

👎 Avoid when

  • General info services on mainstream web/mobile
  • Projects aiming for low cost / fast launch
  • Mass-market services for users without headsets
  • Simple content-consumption apps

🧾Outsourcing brief (Upwork)

Spatial UI — Outsourcing brief (Upwork)

  • For native spatial apps, explicitly require devs with visionOS (SwiftUI/RealityKit) skills
  • For web/mobile ‘depth’ imitation, scope tightly to CSS <code>perspective</code> / <code>transform:translateZ</code> / parallax range in the contract
  • Accessibility: include <code>reduced-motion</code> and motion-sickness mitigation (avoid abrupt depth shifts)
  • Performance: set a frame-rate target (e.g. 60fps) for 3D-layer + blur combos as QA criteria
  • Given hardware dependence, always define fallback UX for non-headset environments
